Enako's Craft Tips

Character study first. Watch every scene your character appears in before designing the costume. Mannerisms inform posing for photography.

Invest in the wig. A cheap costume with a perfect wig looks better than the reverse. Hair styling is 40% of a character's recognition.

Colour-match under convention lighting. Fabrics look different under fluorescent convention lights vs natural light — always test before the event.

Leon's Craft Tips

Build the body, build the character. Physical fitness shapes how costumes drape and how naturally you embody a character. It's part of the craft.

Use thermoplastics for armour. Worbla and Wonderflex are heat-mouldable and create professional armour shapes that foam cannot replicate.

Study the source material's lighting. Animators design characters for specific lighting. Replicate that in your photoshoot for the most iconic result.

Jessica Nigri is one of the most influential figures in Western cosplay history. Beginning her cosplay journey in 2009, she quickly rose to prominence through her bold, creative interpretations of gaming and anime characters. Today she is a professional cosplayer, Twitch streamer, voice actress, and brand ambassador with over 4 million social media followers. Jessica's Craft Tips

Personality is the costume. The most accurate outfit falls flat without the character's energy. Study their personality as much as their appearance.

Collaborate fearlessly. Some of the best cosplay work comes from teaming up with photographers, makeup artists, and prop builders who complement your skills.

Document your build process. Behind-the-scenes content consistently outperforms finished photos. The journey connects with audiences more deeply.

location_on Atlanta, USA  ·  Master Craftsman & Businesswoman

Yaya Han is widely considered the godmother of American professional cosplay. Born in China and based in Atlanta, she has been cosplaying since 1999 and was among the first to turn cosplay into a viable career. Beyond cosplaying, she is an entrepreneur who runs her own fabric and materials brand, Yaya Han Fabric Collection, which supplies cosplayers worldwide. She has appeared on American Heroes Channel's "Heroes of Cosplay" series and judged numerous major international cosplay competitions including Anime Expo and Dragon Con.

Yaya's Craft Tips

Learn to sew properly. Pattern-making and sewing technique are the most transferable skills in cosplay — they apply to every costume regardless of character.

Quality over quantity. One outstanding costume built with care will advance your craft more than ten rushed builds. Patience is a competitive advantage.

Treat cosplay as a business if you go pro. Contracts, invoicing, and personal branding matter as much as craft once you make the leap to professional.
